Streak camera alignment
  1. Limit the pixel intensity to max. 1000 cts/100ms at all times.
  2. Put the camera in focus mode (unchecked).
  3. Turn on the calibration light and close the slit of the spectrograph by toggling the manual focus button. Open the slit and shutter of the streak camera to get the signal in live view. Maximize the intensity by moving the focus of the spectrograph manually. If no signal can be found, most likely the detector offset is completely off. First roughly adjust the detector offset (see next step) and then adjust the spectrograph focus.
  4. Adjust the spectrograph detector offset by adjusting the offset value based on the horizontal position of the spot on the displayed image (change offset using shrkconfig commands in terminal). The spot should be centered on the horizontal wavelength axis (0 pixel). Turn the calibration light off by untoggling the manual focus button.
  5. Optional: Perform a fine spectral calibration by toggling the manual focus button, selecting a grating and setting the center wavelength. Position the peaks of the calibration light (Ar) using the shrkconfig commands in the terminal (see manual: Ar spectrum).
  6. Turn on the e-beam and find the CL spot by adjusting the Y-direction of the FSLT lens


 Trigger delay calibration
  1. Check that the e-beam settings fit the sample.
  2. Load an existing calibration file for a specified e-beam voltage or perform a new trigger delay calibration.
  3. Turn on the e-beam blanker.
  4. Choose the largest time range setting.
  5. Switch on the streak mode (checked).
  6. Optimize the MCP-Gain and the exposure time until the signal (a spot) is sufficient.
  7. Choose a smaller time range if multiple spots are visible.
  8. Center the line by adjusting the trigger delay.
  9. Perform the same procedure for all higher zoom levels.
  10. Save the calibration file specifying the e-beam voltage.
